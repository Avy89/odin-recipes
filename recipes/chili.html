Sweet and Spicy Chili

Enjoy the perfect combo of sweet, smoky, and spicy with this unique chili recipe.

When I was first learning to cook, I started my search for a good chili recipe because I felt like chili was a staple dish that everyone who calls themselves a good cook should know how to make. The problem is, I hate chunks of tomatoes in my food and I quickly learned finding a good chili recipe that doesn't include tomatoes is hard! I tried a few different ones I found before, voila, I found the one.

This chili is perfectly balanced to hit all the sections of your palate. The beef and black beans bring the savory while the baked beans and brown sugar add the sweet. Then, the fresh serrano peppers, chili powder, and cayenne pepper step in to provide the heat you're usually hoping for when you have a bowl of chili. Finally, as an unexpected twist, a hint of smoky paprika comes in to finish off your senses.

Now that your mouth is watering, lets learn how to cook it!

Ingredients

  • 1lb ground beef
  • 1 can of black beans
  • 1 15 oz can of Bush's vegetarian baked beans
  • 29 oz tomato sauce
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 serrano peppers, diced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 3 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper

Steps

  1. In a skillet on medium heat, brown the ground beef along with the diced onions and serrano peppers. During the last few minutes, add in the minced garlic.
  2. Drain the extra grease and dump the beef mixture into your slow cooker.
  3. Add the remaining ingredients into the slow cooker and stir to combine.
  4. Cook on low for 6 hours or on high for 3-4 hours.

Enjoy.
